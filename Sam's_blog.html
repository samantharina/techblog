"Humans Need Not Apply"

by Samantha

The Youtube clip "Humans Need not Apply" talks about the recent demand for robots, and how many jobs are starting to use robots to do tasks. The video explains that robots will soon take over mostly all of our job fields, as they already are, and put many people out of work. The video highlights many jobs that are already using robots (cars, pharmacists, artists) and how it's working smoothly. The video doesn't share any downsides to relying on robots for work, which might show bias on their part.

I believe that although there are many tasks robots can be programmed to do, there are certain jobs that'll always require humans for. For example, entertainment. The entertainment industry relies on people who can sing, act, or use film equipment. Sure, robots can be promgrammed to take over this field, but since everyone has a certain taste and style they prefer, it would be difficult for robots to compete as well.

"Blown to Bits"

by Samantha

The first section I read was titled "Smile While We Snap". It explored photography. It focused on how photographers capture moments that are impactful and have the potential of going viral and catching the publics attention.

The second section I read was titled "Knowing Where You Are" and it described GPS systems. GPS in cars, phones, and other media, and how it "knows" how to track locations and give directions.

The final section was called "The Parking Garage Knows more Than You Think" and it summarized a story of a family who parked their car in a garage, and wasnt allowed to get it back once they were done because the garage had taken a photo of the license plate as it entered.

After reading excerpts from "Blown to Bits" I am fancinated to learn more about technology and realize the functions to things most of us take for granted. It's really cool, but also frightning how we're constantly surrounded by media and it has been the basis for most of our society.

Interview

Sam Silverman

I interviewed Johni. She is a teacher at ACTvF and helps students during class on their codes and websites. Below is my interview with Johni.

Sam: How old were you when you got your first computer?

Johni: I was 12 years old.

Sam: What was your favorite piece of technology when you were growing up?

Johni: My Gameboy.

Sam: How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Johni: Now, a lot more people have technology, as well as knowledge on how to use it.

Sam: Have the changes you've seen in technology over your lifetime mostly helped or hurt society?

Johni: Technology has helped a lot. It's cool how you can stay in touch with someone who is far away.

Sam: What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Johni: To make peoples lives easier and help them stay connected.

Sam: What is your greatest fear for technology in the future?

Johni: It'll make people lonely and depressed.

Sam: What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology?

Johni: To find good content that offensive, ignore trolls, and to help people who are learning about technology.
